Early Game Placement Strategy:

- Aim for an 8-5-10 placement with a secondary placement of 6-9-4 to ensure good number variety.
- Place your first roads strategically to avoid dead ends and ensure future expansion.

Mid-Game Resource Management:
- Ensure access to all needed resources between your two placements.
- Complementary placements are key: if you start with Lumber-Brick-Wheat, aim for Ore and Wool in your next placement.
- Adapt your strategy based on the game phase: prioritize different resources as the game progresses.
- Avoid overproducing resources that can't be used effectively.

Late-Game Victory Point Optimization:
- Don't stress over settlement positioning in the late game; focus on placing them anywhere for victory points.
- Pick up Longest Road as late as possible to avoid becoming a target.
- Use victory point cards to create uncertainty among opponents.

Effective Trading and Negotiation:
- Be flexible in negotiations; consider multi-resource trades if 1-for-1 exchanges aren't suitable.

Development Card Usage:
- Use development cards strategically to gain an advantage.
- Hidden development cards can deter opponents from blocking or stealing from you.
- Leverage the information asymmetry of development cards to drive your strategy.

Robber Placement Strategy:
- Avoid placing the robber on resources you lack; instead, target resources you have in abundance to increase trade opportunities.
- Use the robber on the strongest opponent, not just the open points leader.
- Consider unplayed development cards in an opponent's hand as potential points.
- Use the robber exclusively on your strongest opponent to avoid motivating multiple opponents to seek revenge.
