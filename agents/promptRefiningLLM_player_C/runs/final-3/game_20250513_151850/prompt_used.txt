- Prioritize ore and wheat hexes for your settlements - they enable cities and development cards

- Build at least one new road and one new settlement ASAP
- Focus on securing access to any missing resources through expansion
- Establish access to a port if you're lacking a key resource
- Prevent the opponent from cutting off your expansion path
- Only purchase development cards if excess resources available or for immediate victory
- Expand toward high-value hexes the opponent doesn't yet control

MID-GAME RESOURCE MANAGEMENT (TURNS 9-20):
- Target 3 cities minimum (worth 6 VP) - upgrade most productive settlements first
- Maintain 1 road per every 2 settlements for flexible expansion
- Always build a settlement when you have the resources (more production points)
- Calculate the VP-to-resource ratio for each build (cities give best long-term return)
- Trade aggressively at ports (4:1 or better) to maintain resource balance
- Keep at least one viable road-building path open at all times
- If blocked from expansion, shift to city upgrades and development cards

DEVELOPMENT CARD STRATEGY:
- Development cards should supplement your expansion, not replace it
- Prioritize using Knight cards to protect your best hexes from the robber
- Save Knights when you're ahead to counter the opponent's robber placements
- Only buy development cards when you:
  1. Have excess sheep/wheat/ore but lack wood/brick for expansion
  2. Are close to securing largest army

- Use Road Building cards to save brick/wood and create surprise expansion paths
- Time Monopoly cards to capture opponent's stockpile after high-producing rolls
- Use Year of Plenty to complete crucial builds when short on specific resources

ROBBER PLACEMENT:
- Always target the opponent's highest-producing hex (high number + settlement/city)
- Prioritize blocking ore and wheat hexes to slow city development
- If opponent relies heavily on one resource, target that resource's hexes
- Never place the robber on your own high-production hexes
- When stealing, target resources you immediately need for your next build

VICTORY PATH PLANNING:
- Target resource independence with at least one settlement/city on each resource
- Minimum viable infrastructure: 3-4 settlements total, with 2-3 upgraded to cities
- Calculate multiple paths to 10 VP and pursue the fastest:

- Always track your direct path to 10 VP and focus on those builds
- Prioritize visible points (settlements/cities) over speculative development cards
- Switch paths if the opponent blocks your primary victory path
- Don't appear to be in the lead until you can win in 1-2 turns

TRADING STRATEGY:
- Accept trades only when they enable an immediate, crucial build
- Never make trades that allow opponent to build a settlement or city immediately
- Trade away excess resources when at risk for robber (7+ cards)
- Calculate the production value of each settlement location before deciding to build
- When trading with ports, plan sequences that convert unwanted resources into needed ones

1. Does this move bring me closer to 10 victory points?

3. Does this move protect me from being blocked or cut off?
