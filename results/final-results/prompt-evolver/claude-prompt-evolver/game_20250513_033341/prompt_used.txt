INITIAL SETTLEMENT PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- First priority: Place settlements on high-probability numbers (6 & 8) on different resources
- Target at least one spot with good ore and wheat production for city development
- Try to secure at least one high-probability spot (5, 6, 8, 9) for each critical resource
- Place your second settlement to complement your first (fill resource gaps)
- Aim for access to all five resources or position near useful ports if resource diversity isn't possible
- Always consider which settlements opponents are likely to place and avoid areas that will become contested
- Place roads pointing toward valuable future expansion spots, not toward the center
- Consider the long-term impact: settlements that can't expand are less valuable

MID-GAME RESOURCE PRIORITIES:
- Calculate the VP-to-resource ratio for each possible build (cities: 1VP for 5 resources is most efficient)
- Early phase: Secure at least one good source of brick and wood for minimal expansion
- Growth phase: Prioritize ore and wheat production for city upgrades (3VP for 5 resources is optimal)
- Late phase: Focus on the fastest path to victory points, whether through buildings or development cards
- Avoid trading resources that opponents need for immediate victory point gains
- Use 4:1 trades only when necessary; prioritize port usage when possible

DEVELOPMENT CARD STRATEGY:
- Buy development cards when you have excess sheep/wheat/ore and limited expansion options
- If you get an early knight, save it for defense or until you can start building toward largest army
- Time monopoly card usage for when opponents likely have accumulated specific resources
- Save year of plenty cards for completing crucial builds when you're short 1-2 specific resources
- Development cards are most valuable when:
  * You need hidden victory points to surprise opponents
  * You need knights to break up longest road or secure largest army
  * You're lacking wood/brick for expansion but have plenty of sheep/wheat/ore

EXPANSION STRATEGY:
- Build roads only with clear settlement locations in mind
- Target settlements that give you access to ports or new resources
- Block opponents' expansion paths when it gives you a strategic advantage
- Consider the timing of expansion vs. city upgrades based on your resource production
- Calculate whether longest road is achievable and worth the resource investment
- When deciding between a new settlement and a city upgrade, compare immediate vs. long-term returns

ROBBER STRATEGY:
- Place the robber on the highest-probability hex of the player closest to winning
- Target hexes producing resources you need but can't access easily
- When stealing, prioritize players with cards you need for your next build
- Save knight cards for breaking up opponent strategies or securing largest army
- Use knights defensively if you're producing valuable resources on high-probability hexes

TRADING TACTICS:
- Avoid trading resources that directly help opponents score points
- Use port trading aggressively rather than accepting unfavorable player trades
- Trade excess resources before rolling 7 if you have more than 7 cards
- Consider bank/port trades when they enable an immediate build that advances your strategy
- When trading with players, try to receive resources that complete a build

ENDGAME STRATEGY:
- In the late game, hide your victory point potential (keep development cards hidden)
- Calculate the fastest path to 10 VP and focus exclusively on that path
- Be aware of the exact number of remaining development cards and their probabilities
- Track opponents' potential victory points, including hidden development cards
- If you're close to winning, prioritize actions that can be completed in a single turn

Before finalizing your action, check:
1. Is there a more efficient path to victory points?
2. How will this move position you for the next 2-3 turns?
3. Does this move help or hinder opponents?
4. Is there a defensive move needed to prevent an opponent from winning?
5. Are you properly diversified against dice probability?
