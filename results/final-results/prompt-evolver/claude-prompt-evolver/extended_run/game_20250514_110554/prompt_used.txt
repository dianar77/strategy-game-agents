INITIAL PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- Target hexes with HIGH probability numbers (6,8,5,9) - prioritize these above all else
- Go for resource DIVERSITY: secure ALL five resources between your two settlements
- Focus on ORE and WHEAT for city upgrades (numbers 5-9 preferred)
- Evaluate each potential settlement by its TOTAL PIP COUNT (sum of probability dots)
- Place your initial settlements on OPPOSITE sides of the board to maximize expansion options
- Initial roads should point toward promising expansion spots or ports, NEVER toward the center
- Avoid placing both settlements near the same resources - diversify your income!
- Always consider 3:1 ports if your initial placement gives you limited access to certain resources
- Place roads in directions that block opponents' expansion routes

RESOURCE MANAGEMENT & CITY BUILDING PRIORITY:
- FIRST PRIORITY: Build 1-2 additional settlements early to secure critical resources you lack
- SECOND PRIORITY: Upgrade to cities as quickly as possible to double resource production
- THIRD PRIORITY: Build roads only when they lead directly to valuable settlement locations
- Early game (1-5 turns): Secure key settlements at high-value locations
- Mid-game (6-15 turns): Focus heavily on upgrading to cities (2-3 cities should be your target)
- Late-game: Complete a development card path to victory OR longest road
- When short on a key resource, use 2:1 ports if available before using 3:1 or 4:1 ports
- NEVER trade away ore or wheat to other players unless you're getting ore or wheat in return
- If you have excess of a resource, use ports rather than trading with opponents

DEVELOPMENT CARD STRATEGY:
- Buy development cards primarily when you have excess sheep/wheat/ore
- Save knight cards to protect your high-value hexes from the robber OR to reach Largest Army (3+ knights)
- Use Year of Plenty to get the exact resources needed to complete a city or settlement build
- Use Monopoly cards after high-probability dice rolls to collect maximum resources
- Keep track of how many VP cards you've drawn (5 in the deck total)
- Save victory point cards until you can win in a single turn
- When development cards are your path to victory, aim for: 3 cities (6 VP) + largest army (2 VP) + 2 victory cards (2 VP)
- Consider buying development cards when you have limited build options (blocked roads, etc.)

OPTIMAL EXPANSION PATH:
- Build roads with PURPOSE - every road should lead to a viable settlement location
- Identify and secure "bottleneck" positions that cut off opponents' expansion
- Expand toward resources you LACK from initial placement - seek balance
- When choosing between multiple settlement spots, prioritize:
  1. High-probability production (total pip count)
  2. Access to resources you currently lack
  3. Strategic value (blocking opponents, securing future expansion)
  4. Proximity to useful ports
- If another player is racing you to a spot, calculate if you can win the race before committing
- Longest road (2 VP) requires strategic planning - only pursue if you already have 4+ roads built

ROBBER STRATEGY:
- Always place the robber on the highest-value hex of the player closest to winning
- Target hexes that produce resources you need but can't access easily
- When stealing, prioritize players with cards you need for your next build
- When someone has 8+ points, ALWAYS target them with the robber regardless of other factors
- If you're being targeted by the robber, consider using knight cards immediately to protect production

TURN-BY-TURN PLANNING:
1. Start each turn by assessing your current VP and opponents' VPs (both visible and potential hidden points)
2. Identify your build priority for this turn: settlement, city, road, or development card?
3. Calculate the most efficient path to get required resources for your priority build
4. If you can't build your priority item, determine if trading or buying a development card is better
5. Always make progress each turn - if you can't build, buy a development card or trade for needed resources
6. Anticipate your next 2-3 turns and plan accordingly

WINNING PATHS:
1. CITY-FOCUSED PATH: 3-4 cities (6-8 VP) + 1 settlement (1 VP) + largest army (2 VP) = 9-11 VP
2. BALANCED PATH: 2 cities (4 VP) + 3 settlements (3 VP) + longest road (2 VP) + 1 VP card = 10 VP
3. DEVELOPMENT PATH: 2 cities (4 VP) + 2 settlements (2 VP) + largest army (2 VP) + 2 VP cards = 10 VP

NEVER SAY NO TO FREE VPs:
- Always account for the 2 VP from longest road and largest army in your plans
- These special cards are EASY to lose if you're not defending them
- Invest in roads/knights to maintain your lead if these VPs are part of your strategy
- Be alert for opponents trying to steal these bonus points
