INITIAL PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- First priority: Target high-probability numbers (6 & 8) on different resources
- Aim for resource diversity: Between your two settlements, secure ALL five resources if possible
- Prioritize ore and wheat for city upgrades, then wood/brick for expansion
- Place settlements at intersections of three hexes, maximizing resource production potential
- Place your two initial settlements on OPPOSITE sides of the board to prevent being cut off
- Initial roads should point toward promising expansion spots or ports, not toward the center
- Evaluate each potential settlement location by considering BOTH current value AND future expansion potential
- Avoid placing initial settlements near edges that would limit your expansion options

BALANCED GROWTH STRATEGY:
- Early-game: Build 1-2 additional settlements BEFORE upgrading to cities
- Mid-game: Focus on upgrading settlements to cities and strategic development cards
- Late-game: Complete a development card path to victory OR longest road depending on game state
- Calculate resource efficiency for each build (VP gained per resources spent)
- Monitor opponents' strategies and adjust accordingly - counter their paths to victory!
- Don't over-commit to one strategy - remain flexible based on resource availability

EXPANSION & ROAD STRATEGY:
- Build roads with purpose - every road should lead to a valuable settlement location
- If another player is racing you to a spot, calculate if you can win the race before committing
- Create "road blocks" to cut off opponents' expansion when strategic
- Connect to valuable ports that complement your resource production - a good port can change your strategy
- Secure at least one 3:1 port early if specific 2:1 ports aren't available
- Don't waste resources on roads that don't lead to immediate settlement spots
- Focus expansion toward resource types you lack in your initial placements

DEVELOPMENT CARD STRATEGY:
- Buy development cards strategically, not excessively
- Early development cards: Use only if you lack immediate expansion opportunities
- Mid-game development cards: Use to break resource monopolies or protect key hexes
- Save knight cards to protect your high-value hexes from the robber
- Use Year of Plenty to secure critical missing resources for immediate builds
- Monopoly is most effective when played immediately after a high roll for that resource
- Largest army (2 VP) requires 3+ knights - only pursue if you can maintain the lead
- Victory point cards are most valuable when kept hidden until your winning turn

RESOURCE MANAGEMENT:
- Keep your hand size below 7 cards whenever possible to avoid robber losses
- Don't trade resources that directly enable opponents to build settlements or cities
- Use ports strategically - 2:1 ports are worth 2 resource cards in efficiency
- Calculate the trade-off between immediate builds vs. saving for more valuable builds
- If resource-starved, focus on securing that resource through expansion or trades
- Always make progress each turn - if you can't build, buy a development card

ROBBER PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- Place the robber on the highest-producing hex of the player closest to winning
- Prioritize hexes that produce resources you need but can't easily access
- When stealing, target players with the resources you need most
- Move the robber away from your own high-probability hexes when possible
- Target hexes with multiple settlements/cities to maximize impact
- Consider robber placement that creates favorable trade scenarios

PATH TO VICTORY (PRIMARY):
1. Build 3-4 total settlements in strong positions 
2. Upgrade 2-3 settlements to cities (4-6 VP total)
3. Secure longest road (2 VP) OR largest army (2 VP)
4. Reach 10 VP through a combination of settlements, cities, and special cards

PATH TO VICTORY (ALTERNATIVE):
1. Focus heavily on ore/wheat production
2. Build 2 cities (4 VP) and maintain 2 settlements (2 VP)
3. Buy strategic development cards for hidden VPs and knights
4. Win with combination of cities, settlements, and largest army

BEFORE MAKING A DECISION:
1. Analyze the board state, including all players' visible points and potential hidden points
2. Consider whether your move helps your victory path or hinders opponents
3. Calculate the most resource-efficient build that advances your position
4. Check if you need to protect resources from the robber or other players
5. Balance immediate gains against long-term strategic position
