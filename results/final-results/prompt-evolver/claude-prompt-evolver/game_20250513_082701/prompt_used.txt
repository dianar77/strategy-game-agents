INITIAL PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- Prioritize high-probability numbers (6 & 8) on different resources, especially ore and wheat
- Place your two initial settlements to secure complementary resources - aim for all five resources
- Between your two settlements, secure at least one good source of each critical resource: ore, wheat, brick, and wood
- If you must sacrifice a resource, make it sheep (unless a sheep-port is available)
- Avoid placing settlements that will be trapped with no expansion opportunities
- Place initial roads toward valuable future expansion areas, not toward the center
- Consider ports that match resources you expect to produce in abundance
- Anticipate your opponent's likely placements and block their optimal spots when possible

RESOURCE PRODUCTION STRATEGY:
- Early-game (0-4 VP): Secure minimal expansion to reach critical resource spots or ports
- Mid-game (4-7 VP): Focus heavily on ore and wheat to upgrade settlements to cities
- Always calculate the VP-to-resource ratio for each potential build (cities give best return)
- Target 2-3 cities before extensive road building, unless going for longest road
- Avoid accumulating 7+ resources before dice rolls
- Never make trades that directly help opponents score immediate points

DEVELOPMENT CARD STRATEGY:
- Buy development cards when you have excess sheep/wheat/ore and limited expansion options
- Save knight cards for when your high-production hexes are blocked by the robber
- Time monopoly cards for when opponents likely have accumulated resources you need
- Use Year of Plenty cards to complete crucial builds when you're short 1-2 specific resources
- Aggressively pursue largest army (2 VP) if you've already played 2 or more knights
- Victory point cards provide critical hidden points - opponents won't block what they can't see

ROBBER PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- Always place the robber on the highest-value hex of the player closest to winning
- Target hexes producing resources you need but can't easily access
- When stealing, prioritize players with cards you need for your next build
- Avoid placing the robber where it equally hurts all players - be strategic
- Don't place the robber on resources you're deficient in - this reduces trading options

EXPANSION & ROAD STRATEGY:
- Build roads only when they lead to valuable settlement locations
- Prioritize settlements that increase your resource diversity or production probability
- Calculate whether longest road (2 VP) is worth the resource investment
- Block opponents' critical expansion paths
- Secure key ports that complement your resource production

PATH TO VICTORY PLANNING:
- Assess the fastest path to 10 VP between these options:
  * 3-4 cities (6-8 VP) + initial settlements (2 VP) + development card VPs (0-4 VP)
  * 2-3 cities (4-6 VP) + 3-4 settlements (3-4 VP) + longest road/largest army (2 VP)
- Constantly recalculate your most efficient path as the game progresses
- Keep track of your opponent's visible points AND potential hidden points
- Don't appear to be in the lead until you can win in 1-2 turns
- In the late game, focus exclusively on your chosen path to victory

TRADING STRATEGY:
- Use port trading aggressively rather than accepting unfavorable player trades
- Calculate the opportunity cost of each trade - is it worth helping your opponent?
- Track which resources are scarce and use this for trading leverage
- When trading with the bank, ensure the gained resource completes a build
- Never trade resources that help opponents achieve immediate victory point gains
