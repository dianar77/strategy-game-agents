INITIAL SETTLEMENT PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- First priority: Target high-probability numbers (6 & 8) on different resources
- Aim for access to brick and wood for early expansion, then ore and wheat for city development 
- Place settlements on intersections of complementary resources (ore/wheat or wood/brick)
- Between your two settlements, secure at least one good source of each resource or access to relevant ports
- Place roads pointing toward future expansion opportunities or to block opponent expansion
- Never place settlements that will be "trapped" with no room to expand
- Prioritize nodes where you can access three different resources over nodes with fewer resources
- If you can't get access to all resources, prioritize wheat (most versatile) and ore (for cities)
- When placing second settlement, consider what resources your first settlement is missing

RESOURCE MANAGEMENT STRATEGY:
- Early-game (0-4 VP): Focus on brick and wood to expand to high-value spots quickly
- Mid-game (4-7 VP): Transition to ore and wheat focus for city upgrades (most efficient VP/resource ratio)
- Late-game (7+ VP): Prioritize the fastest path to victory, often through development cards
- Never trade resources that help opponents score immediate victory points
- Use port trading aggressively rather than accepting 4:1 trades
- Calculate the VP-to-resource ratio for each potential build (cities are most efficient)
- Trade excess resources before rolling 7 if you have more than 7 cards
- Avoid over-specializing in just 1-2 resources as this makes you vulnerable to the robber

DEVELOPMENT CARD STRATEGY:
- Buy development cards when you have excess sheep/wheat/ore and limited expansion options
- Save knight cards for defensive purposes to protect high-production hexes
- Time monopoly card usage when opponents likely have accumulated specific resources
- Use year of plenty to complete crucial builds when you're short specific resources
- If you've played 2+ knights, aggressively pursue largest army (2 VP)
- Development cards provide hidden victory points and tactical advantages - buy them when stuck

EXPANSION STRATEGY:
- Build roads only with clear settlement locations in mind
- Prioritize settlements that increase your resource diversity or production probability
- Target settlements near ports that complement your production
- Block opponents' expansion when it threatens your growth opportunities
- Calculate whether longest road is achievable and worth the resource investment
- When deciding between a new settlement and a city upgrade, compare immediate vs. long-term returns
- Position settlements to control key expansion routes, even if initial production is lower

ROBBER PLACEMENT STRATEGY:
- Place the robber on the highest-value hex of the player closest to winning
- Target hexes producing resources you need but can't easily access
- When stealing, prioritize players with cards you need for your next build
- Use knights defensively if you're producing valuable resources on high-probability hexes
- Consider coordinating robber placement to disrupt multiple opponents simultaneously
- Avoid placing the robber where it hurts all players equally - target selectively

OPPONENT MODELING:
- Track which resources opponents are collecting and what they likely need next
- Pay attention to opponents' building patterns to predict their strategies
- Block expansion paths of players who are closest to winning
- Assess which opponent is the biggest threat based on visible points AND potential hidden points
- Don't appear to be in the lead until you can win in 1-2 turns

Before finalizing your action, check:
1. Is this the most efficient path to victory points?
2. How will this position me for the next 2-3 turns?
3. Does this help or hinder opponents?
4. Is there a defensive move needed to prevent an opponent from winning?
5. Am I properly diversified against dice probability?
